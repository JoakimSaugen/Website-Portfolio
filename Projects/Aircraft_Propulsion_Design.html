Battery Pack Integration

My main responsibility at Elfly was the integration of the lithium-ion battery pack into the airframe. This work included:

  • Collaboration with airframe design teams to define integration points, as well as coordinating with the flight physics department regarding CG positioning of the battery.
  • Conceptual development of a mounting solution for the battery pack to meet the CS23 crashworthiness requirements of 18g, as well as serviceability requirements.
  • Designing a thermal runaway system to contain potential thermal runaway events.
×

Electric Motor Cooling

During my time at Elfly, I was responsible for the design of the electric motor oil cooling system. This work included:

  • State-of-the-art research.
  • A MATLAB script for sizing the cooling requirements, required heat exchanger size and drag penalty depending on flight scenario. The script was made to be integratable into a larger aircraft level flight model.
  • Supplier engagement for component sourcing.
×

Charging System

As part of the developemnt of the battery system, I also worked on the charging system. This work included:

  • Conceptual design and dimentioning of ground charging and battery thermal management infrastructure.
  • Development of a tool to estimate turnaround time, based on various input parameters.
  • Sizing and routing of charging cables.

Avionics CAD / Cockpit Mock-up

As part of the role, I was responsible for avionics CAD.

I also worked on the avionics section of the cockpit mock-up, 3D printing mock-ups for the flight deck to allow testing of the aerogenomics and layout of panels and switches.
