Battery Pack Design

Although my years team did not make drastic changes to the battery pack, we still had to manufacture everything to spec, on time, from what documentation was left from the prevoius team.
Althoug the battery pack would not get a mayor upgrade until the next year, we did some incromental improvments, which can be seen bellow:

Pack Configuration

The complete battery pack consists of 6 interchangeable segments arranged to deliver 600V nominal voltage and 7.44 kWh total capacity. Each segment can be individually removed and replaced for maintenance or upgrades.

  • Total Segments: 6
  • Modules per Segment: 4
  • Total Modules: 24
  • Pack Voltage: 600V nominal
  • Pack Capacity: 7.44 kWh
×

Cooling System Integration

The pack-level cooling system integrates forced air ventilation through all segments. Air is drawn from the front and sides of the vehicle, flowing through the 9 cooling channels in each segment.

  • Cooling Method: Forced air convection
  • Total Air Channels: 54 (9 per segment)
  • Channel Diameter: 3.5mm
  • Target Temperature: 25-45°C during operation
×

Interchangeable Modular Design

The battery is designed of 6 interchangeable segments, each consisting of 4 replaceable modules. This ensures excellent reparability if a fault occurs during the season.

×

High Current PCB

Each cell is connected though the high current PCB, Featureing:
- 0.5mm integrated copper busbars
- Laser welded cell tabs for low resistance and high reliability connection
- High accuracy (± 0.8 °C) temperature sensors for each cell pair
- Fused, integrated traces and connection to BMS for voltage reading

×

Cell Structural Fastening

The cell fastning concept was desinged to met the competition requirement for the brackets to hold under a 40g crash, while staying lightweight, non-conductive parts, while at the same time being possible to dissasembel and leaving as much space and surface area as possible for the air cooling. This years desing featured a new UL94 HB rated tape to meet new competition fire retardant requirements, new fillament for the brackets (a UL94 V-0 PC/ABS material), as well as a new UL94 V-0 glass fiber rods to lock the segments into place.

×

Cell Pairing Process

After arriving at our facility, each cell was sorted and paired based on both capacity and internal resistance, to achive the best performance battery on a pack level.

For capacity, the lowest cells were parired with the highest capacity cells to even out the total segment capacity.

As seen in the figure below, cells with unequal resistance will result in a mismatch in cell current within the parallel cell pair. Cells were therefore matched to have as similar resistance within the cell pairs. This mismatch lead to additional current and therefore additional heat generation within the pack.

In addition, in order to achive the best overall pack cooling, cell pairs with higher internal resistance were places in the front and sides, the areas known to have the best air cooling.

×

Integrated Air Cooling

Each segment was integrated with 9, 3.3mm air channels, providing air cooling to both sides of the cell pouch. This years design featured a XX Increase in air channel size.

Development of a New Low Voltage Distribution System

The PCB was designed using Altium Designer. In total, two revisions were made.

The prototype was hand soldered to allow for rapid testing and debugging while building the circuit.
Testing of the voltage output, showing 12V.08V, slightly over to account for cable losses.
Little Woopsie. The footprint of the main DCDC was mirrored in the layout, and one connector did not fit. This is why we test: Fixed for Rev 2.

The previous system had suffered from reliability issues under load and in higher temperatures. The new system was therefore thoroughly tested under load and in climate chamber to ensure reliable operation.

Test setup for system load testing. Fans and power resistors were used for load simulation. Power was supplied by a 600V supply, through a 16:1 DCDC as in the car. An equivalent capacitance was connected to simulate inrush current at start-up.
Climate chamber testing under 60°C to simulate the worst case ambient temperature. A power resistor board, seen in the left lower corner was used as load.

  • System size kept (better EWIS planning)
  • All new power requirements met, while only adding 79 grams
  • No failures of the system during the entire testing and competition season
Final system weight: 238 grams total weight including carbon fiber casing, connectors and cooling.
