Cooling Channels

Cooling routing was switched from a two loop system to a single loop. While this increased ΔT in the loop, this was made up for by a shorter loop, and lower weight. In addition, the hose diameter was reduced from 10mm ID to 8mm ID.

×

Pump Reliability

The previous pump suffered from reliability issues. This was fixed with a combination of fault analysis, pump placement, better mechanical protection of the pump driver, better sealing and air bleeding of the system.

Result: 0 pump realted fautls through the season.

Pump driver in it's own small enclosure for mechancial and water protection.

×

System level optimization

By combining better understanding of the radiator performance through testing, pressure loss vs flow testing of the hose assembly, reduced pressure loss in the motor cooling and better pump integration, the hose assembly could be reduced with 600g, while maintaining cooling performance.

This improvment, although simple, would not have been possible without comprehensive understanding and data about the trade-off each factor like hose diameter, flow rate, and pressure loss imposed on the system performance.

×

Thorough Heat Exchanger Testing

Radiators where thougougly tested to map the performance vs airflow and water flow. This allowed better optimization on a system level.

×

Motor Cooling Jacket

The motor cooling was redesigned to feature 16 parallel channels, turned into the housing body. This resutled in:

  • 2.3x increase in surface area
  • -80% pressure drop though the water path.
  • -46% ΔT between housing body and water
  • No added manufacturing step. Motor housings where already turned
